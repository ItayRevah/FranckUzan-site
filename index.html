The Artist

Trained on monsters,
raised on newsprint.

Franck Uzan is a cover artist working across superhero, kaiju, and pulp horror titles — building bold, poster-worthy compositions designed to sell an issue in half a second flat on a crowded shelf.

The line work is aggressive, the palettes are high-contrast, and every cover is built the way old newsstand books were: one dramatic beat, rendered huge, with zero wasted space.

Equally comfortable inking a street-level noir one-shot and painting a 40-foot kaiju tearing through a skyline — the throughline is always energy, silhouette, and impact.

How a Cover Gets Made
01

Thumbnail

Small, fast composition sketches to lock the silhouette and reading order before any detail goes in.

02

Layout

Tight pencils with lettering zones marked out so the logo and trade dress never fight the art.

03

Ink

Full line work pass — weighted lines, texture, and the claw-mark energy the studio is known for.

04

Color

Flat color, then rendering and light effects, finished with a final contrast pass for shelf pop.
